What is Copyright Infringement?

Copyright infringement occurs when someone exercises one of the exclusive rights of the copyright owner without permission. This includes:

  • Reproducing (copying) the work
  • Publishing the work
  • Performing the work in public
  • Communicating the work to the public (including online)
  • Adapting the work (creating derivative works)

Infringement can be direct (you personally do the infringing act) or indirect (you authorize someone else to infringe, or you provide the means for infringement).

Copyright infringement does NOT require commercial use or profit-making. You can infringe copyright even if you're not making any money from your use of the copyrighted work. Non-commercial infringement is still infringement!

Types of Infringement

Primary (Direct) Infringement

This occurs when someone personally performs one of the restricted acts without permission:

  • Making unauthorized copies
  • Uploading copyrighted material to the internet
  • Playing copyrighted music publicly without a license
  • Screening a film without authorization

Secondary (Indirect) Infringement

This includes:

  • Authorizing infringement - Giving permission or enabling others to infringe
  • Importing infringing copies - Bringing pirated goods into Australia
  • Selling or distributing infringing copies
  • Commercial dealings with infringing copies

Online Infringement

Common forms of online copyright infringement include:

  • Illegal downloading - Using torrent sites, file-sharing platforms
  • Illegal streaming - Watching content on unauthorized streaming sites
  • Sharing - Distributing copyrighted files via email, cloud storage, or social media
  • Posting copyrighted material to websites without permission
  • Screen recording - Recording streaming content

Penalties for Copyright Infringement

Copyright infringement can result in both civil and criminal penalties, depending on the nature and scale of the infringement.

Civil Remedies

Copyright owners can take civil action and seek:

  • Injunctions - Court orders to stop the infringing activity
  • Damages - Financial compensation for losses suffered
    • Can include lost sales, lost licensing fees
    • May include additional damages for flagrant infringement
  • Account of profits - The infringer must hand over profits made from the infringement
  • Delivery or destruction - Infringing copies must be delivered up or destroyed

Criminal Penalties

Serious copyright infringement can result in criminal prosecution, particularly for:

  • Commercial-scale infringement
  • Making or selling pirated copies
  • Operating piracy websites or services

‚ö†Ô∏è Criminal Penalties Can Include:

  • Fines up to $117,000 for individuals
  • Fines up to $585,000 for corporations
  • Imprisonment up to 5 years

Who Can Be Held Liable?

  • Individuals who download, share, or use copyrighted material without permission
  • Businesses that use copyrighted materials in their operations
  • Website operators who host or facilitate access to infringing content
  • Internet Service Providers (ISPs) in some circumstances (though they have safe harbor provisions)
  • Educational institutions if they exceed statutory licensing limits
